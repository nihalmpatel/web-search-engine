Man hospitalized following break-in, assault in Saskatoon

Six people are suspected in a break and enter that sent a 38-year-old man to hospital.

Bryn Levy
Updated: November 29, 2019
Share Adjust Comment Print

A 38-year-old man was seriously hurt in a break and enter at an apartment building in Saskatoon’s Pleasant Hill neighbourhood.

Saskatoon police were called to the building in the 1400 block of 20th Street West around 2 a.m. Thursday with reports that someone was injured.

Officers arrived to find “evidence of an assault,” according to a police news release. However, no victim was found at the scene.

A short time later, the 38-year-old man was found at Royal University Hospital being treated for what were described as serious but non-life threatening injuries.

Police said investigators are looking for six suspects — four men and two women.

The men were described as dressed in black and white clothing with their faces covered. One was wearing a LeBron James basketball jersey. One of the women was reportedly wearing a tan-coloured coat while the other had blue dyed hair.

Anyone with information is asked to contact Saskatoon police at 306-975-8300 or Crime Stoppers at 1-800-222-8477.
