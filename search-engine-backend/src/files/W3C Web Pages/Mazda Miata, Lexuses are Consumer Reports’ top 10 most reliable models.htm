Mazda Miata, Lexuses are Consumer Reports' top 10 most reliable models

Toyota products, an SUV and — wait, a sports car is the most reliable car of the year!?

by Alex Reid   | 4 hours ago
SHARE STORY

Consumer Reports has published its list of the top 10 most reliable vehicle models for 2019, and the one at the very top is, oddly enough, a sports car.

To evaluate the vehicles, Consumer Reports surveys owner on 17 problem areas, including squeaky brakes, broken interior trim, out-of-warranty transmission repairs and four-wheel-drive issues.

The severity of each problem is weighted to give a Predicted Reliability Score, cross-referenced with the staff’s own track tests plus satisfaction survey results to give the final score. Some 420,000 vehicles are surveyed in the test.

Unsurprisingly, the list is populated mostly with vehicles from Toyota and its luxury sub-brand, Lexus. However, Mazda has managed to make great strides, and so has Kia, so let’s take a look.

The Lexus GS sedan is the 10th most reliable car of 2020, followed by the Mazda CX-9. Taking things off-road for number eight is the Toyota 4Runner, and then back to luxury for number seven in the Lexus NX. At number six is another Mazda, the CX-3.

The number five spot is taken by the Hyundai Kona, which also won the North American Utility Vehicle of the year for 2019. The Lexus GX full-sized SUV tumbles a couple of spots from number one, to its new home at number four.

Bronze and silver medals are given to the Prius and Prius Prime, respectively. Thankfully Consumer Reports doesn’t factor in vehicle aesthetics.

Toppling years of Lexus supremacy at the top of the list, the Mazda MX-5 Miata has taken the top spot for most reliable vehicle this year. It comes as a surprise, but makes sense when you think about it. The elemental sports car is almost devoid of electronic intereference – fewer things to go wrong, right? – and with a manual gearbox it drops the complexity down another peg.
